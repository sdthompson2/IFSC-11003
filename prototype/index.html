University of Arkansas at Little Rock

A Brief History

The University of Arkansas at Little Rock (UA Little Rock) is a public research university in Little Rock, Arkansas. Established as Little Rock Junior College by the Little Rock School District in 1927, the institution became a private four-year university under the name Little Rock University in 1957. It returned to public status in 1969 when it merged with the University of Arkansas System under its present name. The former campus of Little Rock Junior College is now (2019) the campus of Philander Smith University.

At 250 acres (100 ha), the UA Little Rock campus encompasses more than 56 buildings, including the Center for Nanotechnology Integrative Sciences, the Emerging Analytics Center, the Sequoyah Research Center, and the Ottenheimer Library. Additionally, UA Little Rock houses special learning facilities that include a learning resource center, art galleries, KUAR public radio station, University Television, and a campus-wide wireless network. It is classified among "R2: Doctoral Universities – High research activity".

Academics

The Univesity features more than 100 undergraduate degrees and 60 graduate degrees, including graduate certificates, master's degrees, and doctorates, through both traditional and online courses.

The student life at UA Little Rock is typical of public universities in the United States. It includes student-run organizations supporting social, academic, athletic, and religious activities. Services offered include intramural sports, fitness programs, diversity programs, leadership development, peer tutoring, student government, student support programs, a student-run newspaper, and fraternity and sorority life. The proximity to downtown Little Rock allows students to take advantage of recreational, educational, internship, and employment opportunities not available elsewhere in Arkansas.

Campus Living

UA Little Rock provides a variety of on-campus living options ranging from traditional resident rooms to multiple-bedroom apartments. There are four residence halls on the eastern campus and the University Village Apartment Complex on the southern side. Six learning communities focus on criminal justice, arts and culture, majors and careers, future business innovators, nursing careers, and STEM.

Athletics

UA Little Rock's 14 athletic teams are known as the Little Rock Trojans, with almost all participating in the Sun Belt Conference. The school last fielded a football team in 1955. Athletic offices are located in the Jack Stephens Center.

Two teams that do not compete in the Sun Belt are Women's Swimming/Diving (Missouri Valley Conference) and Wrestling (Pac-12 Conference). Wrestling started in 2019 as the first Division I program in Arkansas. Little Rock will move to the Ohio Valley Conference for the 2022-23 season.
